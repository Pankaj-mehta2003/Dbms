ROLLBACK

The ROLLBACK command in SQL is used to undo or cancel the changes made by transactions that have not yet been committed to the database. It reverts the database back to the state it was in before the transaction began.

Example:

Step 1: SELECT * FROM DEPT;
DEPTNO DNAME LOC
18 ACCOUNTING NEW YORK
20 RESEARCH DALLAS
30 SALES CHICAGO
40 OPERATIONS BOSTON 
Step 2: INSERT INTO DEPT VALUES (50, 'FINANCE', 'MUMBAI');

1 row created.

Step 3: SELECT * FROM DEPT;
DEPTNO DNAME LOC
18 ACCOUNTING NEW YORK
20 RESEARCH DALLAS
30 SALES CHICAGO
40 OPERATIONS BOSTON
50 FINANCE MUMBAI
NOW YOU DECIDE TO ROLLBACK THE CHANGES. 
Step 4: ROLLBACK;

Rollback complete.

After rolling back the table will revert to its original state.

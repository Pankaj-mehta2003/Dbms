COMMIT

The COMMIT statement is used to make changes (inserts, updates, deletes) permanent in the database. On the other hand, the ROLLBACK statement is used to discard parts or all of the work the user has done in the current transaction, which is important in case an error is detected during DML operations.

Example:

Step 1: SELECT * FROM DEPT;
DEPTNO DNAME LOC
18 ACCOUNTING NEW YORK
20 RESEARCH DALLAS
30 SALES CHICAGO
40 OPERATIONS BOSTON 
Step 2: INSERT INTO DEPT VALUES (50, 'FINANCE', 'MUMBAI');

1 row created.

Step 3: SELECT * FROM DEPT;
DEPTNO DNAME LOC
18 ACCOUNTING NEW YORK
20 RESEARCH DALLAS
30 SALES CHICAGO
40 OPERATIONS BOSTON
50 FINANCE MUMBAI 
Step 4: COMMIT;

Commit complete.

Step 5: SELECT * FROM DEPT;
DEPTNO DNAME LOC
18 ACCOUNTING NEW YORK
20 RESEARCH DALLAS
30 SALES CHICAGO
40 OPERATIONS BOSTON
50 FINANCE MUMBAI

In this example, we first select data from the DEPT table, then insert a new row, and finally commit the transaction to make the changes permanent in the database.

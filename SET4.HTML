UNION ALL

The UNION ALL operator joins the outputs of two or more queries (SELECT Statements) into a single set of rows and columns without the removal of any duplicates.

SYNTAX

            SELECT Column_list from Table1
            UNION ALL
            SELECT Column_list from Table2
            [ORDER BY CLAUSE];

EXAMPLE:

Consider the following table 'EMP':

DEPTNO ENAME JOB
10 CLARK MANAGER
20 SMITH CLERK
20 JONES MANAGER
30 ALLEN SALESMAN
30 JAMES CLERK
To display the different designations in department 20 and 30 of 'EMP' table without removing any duplicate values: 
SELECT JOB FROM EMP
WHERE DEPTNO = 20
UNION ALL
SELECT JOB FROM EMP
WHERE DEPTNO = 30;
OUTPUT:

JOB
MANAGER
CLERK
SALESMAN
CLERK

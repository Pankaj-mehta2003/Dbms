USERDEFINED EXCEPTIONS

In addition to the predefined exceptions, you can also define your own exceptions. Unlike predefined exceptions, user-defined exceptions are declared and defined by the user. User-defined exceptions are declared in the declaration section of PL./SQL block with their type as EXCEPTION. They must be raised explicitly using the RAISE statement, unlike predefined exceptions that are raised implicitly.

Steps to be followed to use User-defined Exceptions:

(i) They should be explicitly declared in the Declaration section. (Declaring an Exception)

(i) They should be explicitly raised in the Execution section. (Raising an Exception)

(iii) They should be handled by referencing the user-defined exception name in the Exception section.

EXAMPLE 


    DECLARE
    v_current_salary NUMBER := 5000;
    v_new_salary NUMBER := 4000;
    invalid_salary_exception EXCEPTION;
    BEGIN
    IF v_new_salary < v_current_salary THEN
                RAISE invalid_salary_exception;
            ELSE
                v_current_salary := v_new_salary;
                DBMS_OUTPUT.PUT_LINE('Salary updated successfully.');
            END IF;
    EXCEPTION
            WHEN invalid_salary_exception THEN
                DBMS_OUTPUT.PUT_LINE('Error: New salary cannot be less than current salary.');
        END;
